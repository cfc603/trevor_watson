Projects

Tool Room

Problem

The tool room at Mercedes-Benz of Daytona Beach offers nearly 500 specialized tools for their technicians to use in their daily repairs. With 17 technicians borrowing and returning the same tools it's easy for them to get misplaced or not returned in a timely manner. Through the day to day operations, there were many times an expensive tool was purchased again, only to find a technician had forgotten to return it.

Solution

A basic web application was developed using Django to store the location of each tool in the tool room. In addition, a check out system was put in place to keep track of each tool as a technician needed it. The checkout system was accompanied by a WiFi enabled lock to give access to a technician to the tool room as they checked in or out a tool.

As feedback was provided by the technicians during the initial launch, it became clear many of the tools were not known by their tool number. An additional iteration was made to allow technicians to tag tools with a keyword or phrase of their choice, to enable quicker lookup and checkout of the tools they used most.

Mercedes Tools

Problem

Through my experience as a Parts Advisor we had a number of resources that were not in the most accessible format. For example, in order to look up a part without the VIN number the car chassis was required which was cataloged in individual PDFs by model year.

Solution

By moving the information to a relational database, accessed through a web app, the information was not only accessible quicker, but also draw connections that were not apparent. With these connections you could show what vehicles shared the same engine or transmission. Identifying parts by engine, such as a oil filter, versus by model allows a Parts Advisor be more efficient in their day to day duties.
